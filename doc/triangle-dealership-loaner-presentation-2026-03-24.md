## Slide 10. Recommended Pricing Strategy

### Title

Competitive pricing model for Triangle

### Recommendation

Use a hybrid pricing structure:

- base monthly fee per rooftop
- includes a block of active loaners
- low overage per additional active unit
- optional add-ons only where value is clear

### Suggested packages

#### Pilot Launch

- `$0` implementation fee for pilot
- `60-day pilot`
- `1 rooftop`
- up to `25 active loaners`
- fixed pilot fee: `$199/month`

#### Core Dealership Loaner

- `1 rooftop`
- includes up to `40 active loaners`
- `$399/month`
- additional active loaners: `$7 per unit/month`

#### Multi-Rooftop Group

- `2+ rooftops`
- includes shared admin rollout
- centralized reporting
- multi-tenant group controls
- custom quote starting around `$699-$1,499/month` depending on scale

### Optional add-ons

- DMS integration project fee
- telematics / GPS integration
- advanced accounting export package
- white-label customer communication templates

## Slide 11. Why This Pricing Can Win

### Title

How to price competitively against the market

### Market read

- ARS publicly shows `$10/VIN/month` with a `$250` monthly rooftop cap
- Dealerware does not show public list pricing, but publicly pushes premium ROI, mobile execution, and transparent invoicing
- RENTALL shows a custom-sales motion and a public minimum size of `10 vehicles`
- Bluebird public pricing is not visible on the pages reviewed

### Recommended pricing posture

- price below premium enterprise players
- price above ultra-budget commodity expectations
- keep invoice simple
- no hidden fees
- offer pilot with low switching risk

### My recommendation

For Triangle specifically:

- lead with pilot
- then target standard pricing around `$299-$399 per rooftop/month`
- if fleet is larger, protect margin with included-unit blocks plus overage

### Presenter note

Esta estrategia te deja:

- más barato y más flexible que premium enterprise
- más moderno y more value-rich than low-cost legacy options

## Market Notes

### Inference

Based on current public materials reviewed on `2026-03-24`, the dealership loaner market appears to break into three practical tiers:

- `legacy / report-heavy`: Bluebird
- `premium modern enterprise`: Dealerware, TSD
- `aggressive SaaS value`: ARSLoaner, likely some RENTALL deals

Ride Fleet can win by positioning itself between premium and value:

- more modern than legacy
- more flexible than enterprise
- more operationally complete than lightweight low-cost tools
